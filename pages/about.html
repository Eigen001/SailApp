The Origin
A photo problem every sailor has

You're heeling hard at 18 degrees, spray coming over the bow, sails perfectly trimmed for the first time all season — and there's nobody to photograph it. Your phone is in a dry bag. The only camera is yours, pointed at the wrong thing.

Meanwhile, the Oyster 53 that just ghosted past you at 8 knots? Her captain got a stunning frame of your boat, perfectly lit, under full sail. He doesn't know who you are. You'll never see that photo.

Broadside fixes that. Every photo taken of every vessel, automatically matched and delivered to the right owner. The community photographs each other, and everyone wins.

We launched on Long Island Sound in the spring of 2024 with 40 vessels. We're now at 4,200+ — and growing across New England, the Chesapeake, and the Great Lakes.

The Technology
AI that understands boats

Our computer vision model was trained specifically on maritime vessels — not repurposed from face recognition or car identification. It understands hull profiles, sail configurations, rigging layouts, cove stripes, and registration numbers at oblique angles from moving platforms.

When a match exceeds 85% confidence, it's delivered automatically. Between 70–85%, it enters a community-assisted review queue where registered vessel owners can confirm or reject. Below 70%, it stays in the community feed untagged — still visible, still shareable.

We sync with AIS transponder data through the MMSI registry, which means we know where registered vessels are in real time and can cross-reference GPS position with photo metadata to dramatically improve match confidence.
